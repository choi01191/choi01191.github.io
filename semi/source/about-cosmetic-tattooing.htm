Cosmetic Tattooing, also known as semi permanent makeup, is a process in which a highly trained technician implants pigment into the epidermal layer of the skin. This can be done using a variety of techniques such as Ombre and Hairstroke to create the desired look. The technician will use thin, medical grade needles and a digital machine to ensure precise implantation of the pigment. The pigment will stay in the epidermal layer of the skin for 2 - 5 years.

 
 
 

Cosmetic tattoos require very little maintanence as only one appointment every 12 - 18 months is necessary to top up the pigment. This is because, over time, factors such as sun exposure, facial treatments and lifestyle may slowly fade the pigment to a lighter shade. A top up of pigment onto the initial tattooed shape and maintains the pigments vibrancy and this can be performed as many times as desired.

Types of Cosmetic Tattoo

Cosmetic Tattoos can be categorised into three types. Hairstroke, Ombre and combination brow. These three unique styles have each been created to produce a specific effect and our technician will discuss all three options with you, taking into account your face and current eyebrow shape, in order to achieve your desired result.

Hairstroke

Used to mimic natural hairs. Perfect for over plucked or sparse brows to fill gaps and create symmetry. This is the most natural style.

Pigments

Pigment quality is often a factor that is overlooked in semi permanent makeup but it can be a critical factor in the longevity and quality of your cosmetic tattoo. Cheap and inferior pigments can cause your tattoo to fade rapidly or even change colour over time. The pigments we use are of the highest standard and are specially formulated from years of research to ensure that your tattoo will continue to look great for a long time to come.

 
 

Colour

Before your treatment begins our technician will mix a pigment colour to match both your hair and skin tone. This colour will be specific to you as no two people are the same. Our technicians are specially trained in colour matching and use their extensive experience to predict exactly how the tattoo will look once healed.
